I went to a cruise to Hawaii. Cruises always keep emergency boats. I saw that kind of a boat hanging near the sidewall of the cruise. I was curious to check that boat. In the middle of the night, I just hopped into that hanging boat and suddenly it has lost the hook and it got dispatched from the cruise. I was left alone in the middle of the sea. I saw an island and I sailed the boat to the island. Luckily, in the emergency boat, there were few emergency things that helped me out such as a knife, fishing net and slipping bag..

Knife

A big old knife. Like one that Crocodile Dundee would have.. And if the knife’s big enough, you can even use it to cut wood for your shelter and camp fires.

Fishing Net

Fish are a very healthy protein choice, and having a net will be sure to make catching them easier. A fishing net is a net used for fishing. Nets are devices made from fibers woven in a grid-like structure. Fishing nets are usually meshes formed by knotting a relatively thin thread.

Sleeping Bag

A sleeping bag is most definitely my least practical choice. My hope is that a sleeping bag will make nights more comfortable and result in a good night’s sleep. It makes my skin crawl to think about sleeping out in the open air without a layer of fabric to protect me from mosquitoes and bats and other nighttime creatures that are lurking around.





Maui Island

Known as the “Valley Isle,” Maui is dotted with quaint towns, artist communities and local favorites that have been around for generations. Maui is the second-largest Hawaiian island, at 729 square miles (only the Big Island of Hawaii is larger). Maui is 48 miles wide east to west, and 26 miles at its greatest north-south dimension. The valley between the west and east halves of Maui is about 6 miles by 6 miles.
About Maui
Maui is an island in the Central Pacific, part of the Hawaiian archipelago.
